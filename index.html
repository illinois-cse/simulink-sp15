    A Short Course for Engineers

    April 20, 22, 27, 29 (Mon/Wed) • 1–3 p.m. • MEL 1001

    MathWorks Simulink is the premier high-powered graphical programming language for modeling and simulating dynamic systems across many domains. This short course is designed to introduce you to the fundamentals of the interface and modeling through engineering exercises and physical models.

    This course will be offered in April 2015 by Computational Science and Engineering for a fee of $200, intended to recover costs of course development and delivery.

    Topics

    1. Introduction to Simulink
      • Control Systems Refresher
      • Interface & workflow
      • Block diagrams
    2. Continuous Systems
      • Comparison & decision states
      • Zero crossings
      • MATLAB functions
      • Subsystems
    3. Discrete Systems
    4. Intermediate Topics
      • Debugging
      • Libraries
      • Large Models
